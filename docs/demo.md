# Demo picks — InsiderBrief

Five company + role combinations chosen for narrative density: recognizable names, rich movement data, interesting counterparties (named companies, not "Stealth Startup"). YC judges will pattern-match instantly.

## The five

| # | Company | Role | Why it demos well |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **OpenAI** | Member of Technical Staff | Verified rich data (3,268 hires, 218 departures). Diaspora to Anthropic / founding own companies — perfect "alumni mafia" story. |
| 2 | **Anthropic** | Research Engineer | Mirror of #1 — shows the OpenAI → Anthropic flow on the *receiving* side. Pair with #1 for a killer juxtaposition. |
| 3 | **Stripe** | Software Engineer | Verified (3,312 hires, 575 departures). Airwallex × 2 = visible competitor poaching. Mature co → richer departure stories. |
| 4 | **Cursor** | Software Engineer | YC-darling, hottest dev tool. Should show heavy recent inflow from Google/Meta — judges feel the velocity. |
| 5 | **Perplexity** | ML Engineer | Fresh hot AI co. Movement is the story (senior poaches from established AI teams). |

## Demo arc

1. Lead with **OpenAI** — strongest visual, instantly recognizable, dense Movement section.
2. Follow with **Anthropic** — same role family, opposite side of the same talent flow. This is the moat moment.
3. If time allows, switch to **Stripe** to prove the product isn't AI-only.

## Pre-demo checklist

- [ ] Run all five queries locally before the demo. Crustdata indexing varies per company name.
- [ ] If **Cursor** returns sparse data, retry as `Anysphere`.
- [ ] Keep backups warm: **Notion**, **Linear**, **Vercel**, **Ramp** — all well-indexed and tell good stories.
- [ ] Verify the Movement strips render with both hires AND departures populated. A strip with only hires (or zero departures after the 18mo client-side filter) loses half the punch.

## What to point out on stage

- The **counterparty company chips** in the strip header — that's the unique signal you can't get from a careers page.
- The **18-month client-side filter** dropping internal title-changes from departures (e.g. for OpenAI, Anna Makanju's Global Affairs → Global Impact title bump is correctly excluded).
- The honest **"per most recent LinkedIn updates"** footnote — credibility move, not a hedge.
